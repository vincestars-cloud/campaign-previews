# Oma Organics - Deep Buyer Research (Suby 10-Tab Framework)

---

## Tab 1: FEARS
- "I have no idea what's actually IN my skincare" -- 70%+ consumers can't decode ingredient lists
- "Am I putting toxic chemicals on my skin every day?" -- parabens, phthalates, sulfates anxiety
- "What if 'natural' is just marketing and this brand is greenwashing me too?"
- "I'm aging faster because of the products I've been using" -- cumulative chemical exposure fear
- "My skin is SO reactive -- everything breaks me out or burns"
- "What if organic products just don't work as well as the chemical ones?"
- "I'm paying premium prices for products that are basically the same as drugstore"
- "The clean beauty industry has NO regulation -- anyone can slap 'organic' on a label"

## Tab 2: HOPES & DREAMS
- "I want to look in the mirror and see glowing, healthy skin -- no foundation needed"
- "I want a simple routine -- 3 products max -- that actually works"
- "I want to trust every ingredient in every product I put on my body"
- "I want my skin to look like I take care of myself, naturally"
- "I want to age gracefully -- not fight aging, but GLOW through it"
- "I want products that smell amazing because of real botanicals, not synthetic fragrance"
- "I want to feel good about what I buy -- for me AND the planet"

## Tab 3: RELATIONSHIP FEARS
- "My friends all have perfect skin -- mine looks tired and dull"
- "My partner notices when I look exhausted. I notice too."
- "I see women my age online who look incredible -- what am I doing wrong?"
- "My daughter has better skin than me using $12 products"
- "I don't want to be the woman who 'let herself go'"
- "People assume I'm older than I am -- I can see it in their faces"

## Tab 4: RELATIONSHIP SOUNDBITES
- "You look tired today" -- the phrase every woman dreads
- "What's your skincare routine?" -- asked by someone with visibly better skin
- "I stopped wearing makeup and my skin actually got better"
- "My mom always said just use soap and water -- but that doesn't work anymore"
- "My dermatologist told me to stop using everything with fragrance"
- "I spent $200 on a serum that did NOTHING"

## Tab 5: FRUSTRATIONS
- "I've tried SO MANY products -- nothing works for more than a week"
- "Every brand claims to be 'clean' -- I can't tell who's lying"
- "The ingredient list has 40 things I can't pronounce"
- "Natural products spoil too fast or separate in the bottle"
- "I want organic but I'm not paying $90 for a moisturizer that doesn't work"
- "Every time I try something new, my skin freaks out for 2 weeks"
- "The 'clean beauty' brands at Sephora still have questionable ingredients when you dig"
- "I don't have time for a 10-step Korean skincare routine"

## Tab 6: PREVIOUS SOLUTIONS
- Drugstore brands (CeraVe, Neutrogena) -- "too many chemicals, broke me out"
- Prestige brands (Drunk Elephant, Tatcha) -- "expensive, marketed as clean but aren't fully"
- True clean brands (Tata Harper, Herbivore) -- "nice but didn't see real results"
- DIY/homemade -- "coconut oil clogged my pores, essential oils irritated my skin"
- Dermatologist recommendations -- "Retin-A works but my skin is SO dry and flaky"
- Subscription boxes -- "got overwhelmed with products I never used"
- "Clean at Sephora" section -- "still has parabens if you read closely"

## Tab 7: SOLUTION SOUNDBITES
- "I wish there was a brand I could trust completely -- every ingredient, every product"
- "I wish I could feel the difference, not just read about it"
- "I wish 'organic' actually meant something -- like USDA certified, not self-labeled"
- "I wish I could have ONE brand for everything -- face, body, all of it"
- "I wish someone would just make it simple -- here's what works, use this"
- "I wish my skincare smelled like a garden, not a lab"

## Tab 8: DESIRED OUTCOMES
- Visible glow within 2-4 weeks (the "your skin looks amazing" compliment)
- Clear, even skin tone without foundation
- Hydration that lasts all day -- not just the first hour
- Fine lines that look softer, not "filled" -- natural anti-aging
- Zero irritation, redness, or breakouts from new products
- Simplified routine: cleanse, nourish, protect -- done
- Feeling PROUD of what's in their products (ingredient transparency)
- Skin that looks healthy from the inside out -- not "covered up"

## Tab 9: TYPICAL DAY
6:00 AM -- Looks in mirror. Notices new fine line. Sighs. Reaches for moisturizer -- reads the back. Can't pronounce half the ingredients. Uses it anyway.
7:30 AM -- Puts on foundation to look "alive." Wonders when she started needing this much coverage.
12:00 PM -- Skin feels tight and dry by lunch. The $65 serum isn't doing anything.
2:00 PM -- Sees an Instagram ad for a "clean" brand. Clicks. Reads ingredients -- still has phenoxyethanol and dimethicone. Closes tab.
4:00 PM -- Coworker compliments another woman's skin. "What do you use?" She says some brand she's never heard of.
7:00 PM -- Washes face. Skin looks red and uneven. Applies night cream that pills under her eyes.
9:00 PM -- Scrolls skincare TikTok. Everyone seems to have found "the one." She's still searching.

## Tab 10: BUYER SUMMARY
**Demographics:** Women 25-50. Urban/suburban. Household income $60K-$150K+. Educated (college+). Health-conscious. Shops both online and at specialty retailers.

**Psychographics:** Values ingredient transparency. Skeptical of marketing claims. Willing to pay premium IF it genuinely works. Cares about sustainability but won't sacrifice efficacy. Influenced by peer recommendations more than ads. Reads labels. Researches before buying.

**Buying triggers:** Bad reaction to current product, friend recommendation, "glow" compliment envy, turning 30/40 (milestone anxiety), pregnancy/postpartum (ingredient awareness spikes), learning about a specific harmful ingredient.

**Decision maker:** Almost always the woman herself. Gift-givers (partners, mothers, daughters) are secondary -- they buy based on what SHE has mentioned wanting.
