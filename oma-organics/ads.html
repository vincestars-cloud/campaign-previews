Quick-reference for the person building and launching these ads. Based on $200M+ in tested ad spend data (Suby, Hormozi, Cottrell).

The Hyperdopamine Formula

Pattern Interrupt + Burning Intrigue + Big Specific Benefit = HYPERDOPAMINE AD

Your ad competes with Netflix, TMZ, and UFC — not just other skincare brands. 98.1% of Facebook Feed has NO external links. Make ads that look like organic content, not ads.

6 Creative Styles — Every Ad Should Be Produced in Multiple Formats

1. Raw Native
iPhone photo, messy bathroom counter, real lighting. Looks like a friend's post, NOT an ad. Best performer.
2. SMS/Screenshot
Text message thread: “OMG what is this oil?? My skin is GLOWING.” Pattern interrupt because it looks personal.
3. Breaking News
“BREAKING: 73% of 'clean' skincare contains undisclosed chemicals.” Safest bet — works in EVERY market.
4. Native Highlight
“See this? That's a forever chemical.” Real photo + red circle/arrow on ingredient list. Instant curiosity.
5. Native Social Post
Mimics organic post format. Primary image + secondary image. Forces brain to process 2 things = scroll stop.
6. Secret Info
“The ingredient list Sephora's 'Clean' section doesn't want you to see.” Maps, data, insider reveals.

Rules for the Growth Operator

→ 70/20/10: 70% reskin winners, 20% adjacent, 10% wild ideas
→ Positive > Negative: Benefit hooks beat pain hooks 8/10 times
→ 5th grade: Write at grade 3-5 reading level (Hemingway App)
→ 2200 chars: Max for cross-platform (FB + IG)
→ Learn More: Only CTA that works. Never “Buy Now”
→ Dual images: Primary + secondary image stops scroll 2x
→ Lead-in copy: Most overlooked element. Can 5x ROAS alone
→ Proof = 80%: 80% of resources should go to proof-based creative

Winner Reskinning Protocol (Post-Launch)

When Meta tells you which ad wins, create 100+ variations before moving on:

• B&W / sepia / warm / cool filters
• Different headline fonts & sizes
• Swap hooks (keep body)
• Same visual, different text overlay
• Same format, different customer story
• Crop variations (1:1, 4:5, 9:16)
• Voice-swap (different composite character)
• Reorder body copy sections
