WHAT TO EXPECT

What Makes Our Illustration Different From What You Have Seen Before

1. Plain English, Not Industry Jargon

Most annuity illustrations are 30-page documents packed with actuarial tables, surrender schedules, and legal footnotes that require a finance degree to decode. Ours is different.

We start with a one-page summary showing your monthly income, your total annual income, and how it compares to your current expenses. The detailed projections are included for those who want them -- but the first page tells you everything you need to know in under 60 seconds.

2. Multiple Scenarios, Not Just One

Your illustration will include at least three scenarios: the conservative option (maximum guaranteed income, lower growth), the balanced option (moderate income with some growth potential), and the growth-focused option (lower initial income with upside potential).

This way, you are not choosing between "annuity or no annuity." You are choosing which version fits your specific risk tolerance, income needs, and legacy goals. Most people find the balanced option is exactly what they needed -- enough guaranteed income to cover essentials, enough growth potential to fund the retirement lifestyle they have been dreaming about.

3. The "Do Nothing" Comparison

We include a fourth scenario that most advisors skip: what happens if you do nothing and continue with your current withdrawal strategy. This shows your projected income under favorable, average, and unfavorable market conditions.

For many people, seeing the "do nothing" scenario next to the guaranteed scenarios is the moment of clarity. Not because the guaranteed option always wins on returns -- but because it wins on certainty. And at this stage of life, certainty has a value that cannot be measured in basis points.

4. Full Fee Transparency

Every fee is listed. Every charge is explained. No asterisks, no "see page 47," no surprises buried in the fine print. You will know exactly what you are paying and exactly what you are getting in return.

If the fees do not make sense for your situation, we will tell you. We would rather lose a potential client than damage our reputation by recommending something that does not clearly benefit you. That is what fiduciary duty means.
