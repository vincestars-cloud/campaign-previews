THE 3 OPTIONS

Three Types of Guaranteed Income -- Each Built for a Different Goal

Not all annuities are created equal. Here is how to know which one fits your situation.

GROWTH + PROTECTION

Fixed-Index Annuity (FIA)

Grow when markets go up. Lose nothing when they crash.

Your returns are linked to a market index (like the S&P 500) but your principal is protected from losses. You capture a portion of the upside with zero downside risk. Optional lifetime income rider guarantees a monthly check for life.

BEST FOR

People 55-65 who want growth potential without the risk of losing money. Ideal if retirement is 5-10 years away.

GUARANTEED RATE

Multi-Year Guaranteed Annuity (MYGA)

Like a CD. But with a better rate and tax advantages.

A fixed interest rate for a set period (3-10 years). Your rate is locked in the day you purchase, regardless of what happens in the economy. Tax-deferred growth means your money compounds faster than in a taxable CD.

BEST FOR

Conservative savers who want predictability. Currently offering rates that beat CDs by 0.5-1.5%. Great for the "safe money" bucket.

LIFETIME PAYCHECK

Income Annuity (SPIA / DIA)

A paycheck that never stops. Ever.

You convert a portion of your savings into a guaranteed monthly payment for life. No market risk. No withdrawal decisions. No monitoring. The insurance company takes on all the longevity and investment risk. You just deposit the check.

BEST FOR

People 62+ who want to cover essential expenses with guaranteed income, freeing up other assets for growth and "fun money."

THE STRATEGY

The "Floor and Upside" Approach That Changes Everything

Here is the key insight most financial advisors miss: you do not need to choose between guaranteed income and market growth. The smartest retirees use both.

Step 1: Build the floor. Use a portion of your savings (typically 30-50%) to create guaranteed monthly income that covers your essential expenses -- housing, food, utilities, healthcare, insurance. This money arrives every month no matter what.

Step 2: Invest the upside. The remaining 50-70% of your portfolio stays invested in the market. Stocks, bonds, ETFs -- whatever you prefer. This is your growth bucket, your travel bucket, your "fun stuff" bucket.

The psychological shift is profound. When your essentials are already covered, a market crash becomes an inconvenience instead of a catastrophe. You stop panic-selling because you do not need that money to eat. You might even see the crash as a buying opportunity.

BEFORE YOU COMPARE

The 3 Most Expensive Mistakes People Make When Choosing an Annuity

MISTAKE #1

Buying From a Commission-Based Agent

The biggest annuity horror stories come from people who bought from insurance salespeople disguised as "financial advisors." These agents earn 5-8% commissions on variable annuities -- which is why they recommend them even when simpler, cheaper products would serve you better.

The fix: Only work with fee-only fiduciary advisors who have no commission incentive. Our calculator connects you with advisors who are legally required to act in your best interest.

MISTAKE #2

Putting Too Much Money In

Some advisors will try to move your entire portfolio into an annuity. This is almost always wrong. You need liquidity for emergencies, home repairs, medical expenses, and opportunities. The right amount is typically 30-50% of your total savings -- enough to cover essentials, not so much that you feel locked in.

The fix: Our calculator shows you exactly how much of your savings should go toward guaranteed income -- and how much should stay invested for growth.

MISTAKE #3

Comparing Annuities to the S&P 500

This is the most common mistake smart investors make. They look at annuity returns and say "I can do better in the market." And they are right -- in a good decade. But an annuity is not competing with the market. It is competing with the worst-case scenario. It answers a fundamentally different question: "What is the minimum I will receive every month, no matter what?"

The fix: Compare an annuity to the alternative it actually replaces: the anxiety of withdrawing from a declining portfolio, the risk of running out, the sleepless nights wondering if the market will recover in time.

THE REFRAME

Stop Thinking of It as an Investment. Start Thinking of It as Insurance.

You insure your home even though you do not expect a fire. You insure your car even though you plan to drive carefully. Why would you leave your retirement income uninsured?

A guaranteed income annuity is not an investment product. It is an insurance product. It insures against the one risk that can destroy an otherwise successful retirement: living longer than your money lasts.

When you reframe it this way, the question changes. Instead of "Can I get a better return?" the question becomes: "What is the cost of NOT insuring my income?"

For most people, the answer is: decades of unnecessary anxiety, lower quality of life, delayed retirement, and the constant fear that one bad sequence of market returns could undo everything. That is an expensive premium to pay for doing nothing.
