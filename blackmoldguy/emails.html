"Why bleach doesn't actually fix mold"
The honest answer most contractors won't give you...

Hi [First Name],

One of the most common things I see: a homeowner scrubs the mold with bleach, it disappears... and then comes back 3 weeks later in the same spot. I want to explain why, because most people think they did something wrong.

They didn't. Bleach is the wrong tool.

Here's the science in plain English: Bleach is 94% water. On porous surfaces — drywall, wood, grout, concrete — the water soaks in but the bleach evaporates before it penetrates. The surface looks clean. The mold root system (called hyphae) survives below the surface and regrows.

"I scrubbed it with bleach twice. Two weeks later it was back, bigger than before."
— Real homeowner, Reddit

This is also why many remediation jobs fail the first time — if the underlying moisture source isn't addressed and the right chemistry isn't used, mold comes back.

When you're ready to do this right the first time, I'm here.

"What most people forget to ask a mold company"
5 questions every homeowner should ask before hiring anyone...

Hi [First Name],

If you're comparing options or getting multiple quotes, I want to make sure you're protected. The mold industry has a serious conflict-of-interest problem that most people don't know about until after they've been burned.

Here are 5 questions to ask any mold company before hiring them:

1. "Do you also do remediation?"
If yes — and they're the ones inspecting — that's a conflict of interest. They profit more if they find mold. Several states have made this illegal.

2. "Are your results from a third-party lab?"
In-house testing can be manipulated. Legitimate results go to an independent certified lab.

3. "Will you provide a written report I keep regardless of whether I hire you?"
Any reputable inspector says yes.

4. "What training or certifications do you have?"
Look for IICRC certification (mold remediation standard).

5. "Can I see reviews where customers describe your results — not just your communication?"
Communication reviews are easy. Results reviews prove the work actually held.

We pass all five. Happy to show you our certifications and lab reports from past jobs.
