Common Questions

Straight Answers. No Fine Print Surprises.

What exactly is a Retirement Growth Plan?
It's a financial contract backed by A-rated insurance institutions. Your principal is protected from market losses. When the market goes up, you earn a portion of the gains (up to a cap). When the market goes down, you earn 0% — not negative. Think of it as a bond alternative with upside potential. It is not a savings account, not a CD, and not a stock.
How is this different from just keeping my money in CDs?
CDs pay a fixed rate (currently 4–5%) with no upside if markets rise. The RGP offers principal protection like a CD, but with the potential to earn more in years when the market performs well. Plus, growth is tax-deferred — you don't pay taxes on gains each year like you would with CD interest.
Can I actually lose money?
Your principal is protected from market losses. In a year where the market drops 30%, your account earns 0% — not negative 30%. The floor is zero. The only way to incur a penalty is withdrawing more than the penalty-free amount during the surrender period.
What about the "10.5%" — is that my guaranteed annual return?
No, and we want to be completely transparent about this. That figure represents the cap rate on certain products — meaning in a year where the market gains 15%, you'd earn up to 10.5%. In a year where the market gains 5%, you'd earn 5%. In a down year, you'd earn 0%. Over a full market cycle, realistic average returns range from 4–7% depending on the product and market conditions. We'll show you the actual math for your specific situation.
Can I access my money if I need it?
Most plans allow you to withdraw 10% of your account value each year with no penalty. Many contracts also include waivers for nursing home stays, terminal illness, or other qualifying hardships. However, this is designed for money you won't need for 7–10 years. That's why we recommend having separate liquid savings for short-term needs.
What are the fees?
Most fixed indexed products have zero annual management fees. There are no advisor fees taken from your account. The insurance company earns from the spread between what they invest and what they credit to you. Compare that to a traditional financial advisor charging 1–2% per year on your balance.
Is this an IUL (Indexed Universal Life)?
No. This is not life insurance. An IUL has mortality charges and insurance costs that eat into your returns. The Retirement Growth Plan is a different category of financial contract — focused purely on protected growth and retirement income.
How do I know this isn't too good to be true?
Because we're telling you the tradeoff upfront: you get safety and growth, but you give up full liquidity for 7–10 years. Any product promising all three — high returns, zero risk, AND full access — is lying. We'd rather have an honest 15-minute conversation than waste your time with hype.
