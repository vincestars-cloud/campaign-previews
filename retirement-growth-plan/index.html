Common Questions

Straight Answers. No Fine Print Surprises.

What exactly is a Retirement Growth Plan?
It's a financial strategy backed by A-rated institutions that protects your principal from market losses. When the market goes up, your money participates in the gains. When the market goes down, your account doesn't lose value. Your Licensed Growth Plan Advisor will explain the specific structure and options based on your situation.
How is this different from keeping my money in CDs or bonds?
CDs and bonds pay fixed rates with no upside when markets rise. The RGP offers principal protection with the potential to earn more in years when the market performs well. Your advisor will show you a side-by-side comparison specific to your savings level so you can see the actual difference.
Can I actually lose money?
Your principal is protected from market losses. In a year where the market drops 30%, your account doesn't go backwards. The floor is zero — not negative. That's the core of what makes this different from traditional market investments.
What kind of growth can I realistically expect?
Historical returns vary depending on the specific plan and market conditions. Your Licensed Growth Plan Advisor will walk you through realistic projections based on your savings, your timeline, and the current rate environment — so you know exactly what to expect before making any decisions.
Can I access my money if I need it?
Yes. Most plans offer access to a percentage of your principal each year. There are also plans with additional flexibility depending on your needs. Your advisor will help match you with the right plan structure based on your liquidity requirements.
Are there fees?
The fee structure varies by plan. Many options have zero annual management fees — unlike traditional financial advisors who charge 1–2% per year on your balance. Your advisor will break down the complete cost structure so there are no surprises.
Is this an IUL (Indexed Universal Life)?
No. This is not life insurance. The Retirement Growth Plan is a separate category of financial strategy focused on protected growth and retirement income — without the mortality charges that come with insurance products.
How do I know this isn't too good to be true?
Because we don't promise everything. Every financial strategy involves choices. Your advisor will be transparent about what this does well, where the tradeoffs are, and whether it's actually the right fit for your specific situation. If it's not a fit, they'll tell you.
