Agency Industry Context

The Blame Game

  • Client fires the agency, not the vendor. “It wasn’t the script. It wasn’t the reps. The list was garbage.” — r/leadgeneration
  • Month 1: onboarding. Month 2: campaign launches. Month 3: client is already shopping for a new agency.
  • “Those lead sellers sell mostly stolen contacts or resell contacts — worth almost nothing.” — r/leadgeneration
  • The agency takes the blame for data they didn’t choose, from a vendor they didn’t vet

Multi-Vendor Nightmare

  • 3 vendors, half overlap, 2 weeks cleaning per client
  • Nobody guarantees anything — vendors sell and disappear
  • Dedup, skip trace, format conversion — 40 hours of unbillable work per new client
  • Every vertical needs different data: solar, roofing, HVAC, insurance — different vendors for each

SMS & Deliverability

  • 10%+ error rates lock SMS accounts, carrier flags poison all future campaigns
  • The vendor’s name isn’t on the suspension notice. The agency’s is.
  • One bad list tanks sender reputation across every client campaign

Inconsistent Results

  • January: 47 conversations. February: 14. Same strategy, same team, same scripts.
  • Can’t put numbers on proposals because results are unpredictable
  • Client retention collapses when month-to-month results swing wildly

Target Audience

  • Lead gen agency owners, cold calling agency operators, marketing agency founders running outbound for clients
  • Pain: client churn from bad data, unbillable hours, inconsistent results, multi-vendor chaos
  • Landing page: solidbookedpro.com
