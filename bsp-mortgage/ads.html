Mortgage Industry Context

The Trigger Lead Ban (March 5, 2026)

  • Homebuyers Privacy Protection Act banned trigger leads — the mortgage industry’s highest-intent, lowest-cost lead source for 15 years
  • Pre-ban: $0.10-$0.50/lead with real-time credit-pull intent signals. Post-ban: $30-$150/lead from aggregators with shared, recycled data
  • Pre-ban economics: $14K/month on triggers → 24 closings/month → $115,200 revenue. Post-ban: $18.5K/month on aggregators → 11 closings/month → $52,800 revenue
  • Cost UP 32%. Revenue DOWN 54%. Every LO in America competing for the same shared leads from the same 3 aggregators

The Shared Lead Math Problem

  • Aggregators: $30-$150/lead, 0.5-2% close rate = $3,000-$15,000 cost per funded loan
  • Premium shared: $100-$250/lead = $3,750-$6,250 per funded loan
  • MBA data: $11,000-$12,500 to originate one loan, net profit $443
  • “Buying mortgage leads as a rookie is a quick way to go broke” — r/loanoriginators

Dead Numbers & Speed-to-Call

  • 60-70% skip trace accuracy = up to 30% wrong/disconnected numbers
  • Shared leads: borrower gets 5 calls in 12 minutes. You’re call #4.
  • $43,000 TCPA fine per violation on DNC numbers. LOs dialing without DNC status.
  • Top producers leaving because they dial dead numbers all day

Target Audience

  • Loan officers, branch managers, mortgage ops leads, call center managers
  • Pain: pipeline collapsed overnight, shared leads hemorrhaging money, reps quitting, can’t forecast
